a tumbleweed information page

The tumbleweed might bring to mind images of dueling cowboys, swinging saloon doors and arid ghost towns,
but it was once foreign to the American West.
While several plant species form tumbleweeds, the best known is the Rusian Thistle,
said to have snuck into South Dakota in the pouches of Russian immigrants carrying flax.
It is a bushy, spiny plant, with pin-thin leaves and pinkish blooms from summer to fall.
When the plant dies in winter,
the dry, bristly top
breaks
from the root
and is driven by the wind.
As the plant rolls,

it scatters seeds,

displacing native plants ...

until it is stilled.

Seldom the first choice of food for wildlife,
it has proved vital to the ecosystem,
sustaining starving animals through the
drought and dust storms of the 1930s.
